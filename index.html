A Legacy of Fire & Butter

The Authentic Taste of Davanagere in Bengaluru

In an era of fast food and automated assembly lines, we stand by the fire. Our kitchen is built around heavy cast-iron tavas heated exclusively by wood embers. This isn't just about preserving tradition—it's about the unique heat curve that only wood fire can provide, giving our Benne Dose its signature texture: a thin, golden-crisp exterior that yields to a pillowy, soft interior.

Every single day, we source pure butter from local dairies, ensuring the exact moisture content needed to melt perfectly into the steaming hot batter. We serve it with our iconic, fiery-yet-balanced Davanagere Potato Palya and freshly ground coconut chutney. No synthetic ingredients, no shortcuts. Just honest food prepared with absolute craftsmanship.

The Craft

How Your Benne Dose is Crafted

Four steps executed with traditional discipline to bring authentic Davanagere taste to your table.

01

Natural Fermentation

We stone-grind locally sourced rice and split black gram daily, letting the batter ferment naturally for 12 hours to achieve the perfect tanginess.

02

Wood-Fire Cast Iron

Our heavy cast-iron griddles (tavas) are heated using dry logs. The intense, steady heat of the wood logs creates a signature smoky crust.

03

Fresh Butter Glaze

We spread a generous scoop of pure unsalted local butter directly onto the dosa pores as it cooks, infusing it with flavor from edge to edge.

04

Served Immediately

Slipped hot off the iron tava directly onto your plate, accompanied by our signature spicy aloo palya and cool coconut chutney.
